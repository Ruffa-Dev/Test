Curious how it all works?

Simple on the outside, complex on the inside. There is a lot of ‘magic’ happening to keep your data private, safe and ready to use.

1

Upload

During upload your data is split into chunks and encrypted for security. It’s then distributed across the Hive network.

Chunk

Hive splits files into small pieces. This allows better performance as bandwidth demands are split across the network and can be downloaded in parallel later.

Compress

Chunks are compressed with the zlib algorithm to reduce their size.

Encrypt

Client-side encryption is applied to the chunks. It means Hive cannot know anything about the stored data. It uses AES-GCM with a 256 bit key. This is considered quantum-safe.

Shard

Encrypted chunks are split further into shards. This prepares the data for the next steps.

Encode

Shards are encoded using erasure codes (Reed-Solomon codes to be precise) which results in additional shards being created. This allows to retrieve the original data with only 50% of available shards.

Select nodes

In preparation to disseminate the data across the Hive network (HiveNet), most appropriate nodes are selected based on reliability, capacity, performance as well as location to reduce long-distance travel.

Disseminate

HiveDrive uploads the required number of shards to selected nodes.

2

Store

Your data is resilient to nodes disappearing from HiveNet. It’s being constantly monitored so that you can get your data from anywhere at any time.

Monitor node availability

The network keeps monitoring the redundancy ratio of your data by calculating the number of available shards for each of your files. The redundancy ratio is calculated as the total number of available shards divided by the number of required shards to reconstruct your original data

Heal and regenerate

If the redundancy level goes under a certain safety threshold, then the network triggers a healing process.
The swarm healing process applies the ECC encoding process on the available shards. This operation regenerates new shards in order to keep the redundancy ratio of your data at the desired level. The swarm healing process is zero-knowledge since the network processes and generates encrypted shards.

Redistribute

Once the new shards are generated, the network proceeds with a node selection to select additional ones for hosting your data and disseminates the new shards on those nodes.

3

Download

Your data is resilient to nodes disappearing from HiveNet. It’s being constantly monitored so that you can get your data from anywhere at any time.

Download chunks

HiveDrive starts by downloading in parallel the minimum subset of shards needed to decode the chunks.

Decode

The second operation consists in recalculating the missing shards based on the downloaded ones.
The decoding process generates encrypted and compressed chunks.

Decrypt

To decrypt the chunks we need first to retrieve the encryption keys from the network.
Just as in the upload process, the entire process is done with zero-knowledge end to end encryption that happens on the client side. That means decryption happens only on your device, and no other node not even Hive can access your data.

Decompress

Decrypted chunks are decompressed.

Reconstruct

They are then recombined to reconstruct the original file. You can now click on the file to open it.
